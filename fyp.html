    Sky Farm Island

    Overview

    About

    Sky Farm Island is a farming simulation game built on the Roblox platform. In the game, players manage their own crops and trade goods, all while optimizing limited resources. The game mirrors real-world sustainable practices, encouraging players to think critically about food production and environmental conservation.

    Team Size

    • A Group of 5 (Separated into Website and Social Media Teams)

    Role

    • UX Researcher
    • UX for Social Media (Instagram)

    Toolkit

    • Adobe Illustrator
    • Miro
    • Pencil and Paper

    Problem Statement

    • Lack of Engagement

    Research

    How-Might-We Statement

    How might we improve digital platforms used to recommend Sky Farm Island to Parents and Educators, so that the level of engagement from children aged 8 to 12 increases?

    Methodologies

    • Survey
    • Interview

    Research Goal

    • Gather insights from target audience about Sky Farm Island
    • Past and Present expectations for Sky Farm Island (Before and After Comparison)
    • How we can improve Sky Farm Island’s Social Media presence

    Methodologies Overview

    There were a total of 50 respondents, a mixed of teachers and parents. The survey was distributed in Republic Polytechnic, Telegram Channels, and Client's Network.

    Survey Insights

    • 71.4% of respondents with children or students aged 8–12 expressed interest in introducing educational games on Roblox.
    • Only 30% of respondents follow Sky Farm Island on social media, with Instagram being slightly more popular than TikTok.
    • Most Popular Content was Gameplay Tips and Tricks.

    Follow-up Survey Insights

    • 50.0% found the game visually appealing, 66.7% thought the design seemed outdated, 33.3% found the interface confusing.
    • Social Media Platforms religiously used and preferred were Instagram (100.0%), Facebook (83.3%), LinkedIn (66.7%).

    Usability Testing

    Test Plan

    Purpose: To conduct a usability study to understand the following:

    • The usability issues in the latest website
    • What the target audience think about the usability of the updated website
    • Whether the audience knows how to go about SFI’s website

    Test Goals

    1. Check one of Sky Farm Islands' Instagram (Contact Us)
    2. Sign up for Microgreens Workshop (Events)
    3. Play Sky Farm Island (Home)
    4. Watch Roblox Video (Home)
    5. Find SFI's Vision and Mission (About Us)

    Test Participants Profile: Parents and Educators with kids aged 8 to 12

    • Must be able to read and speak in English
    • Must have a computer
    • Must NOT have any experience with using Sky Farm Island's current website
    • Must have basic knowledge of technology
    • Must have and/or teach children of aged 8-12
    • Must have MSTeams

    Number of Test Participant

    • 3 Parents
    • 3 Educators

    Length of Test Session: A total of 45 minutes

    • Briefing: 3min
    • Test Session: 35mins
    • Post-Test Questions: 5mins
    • Debrief: 2mins

    Location and Environment Set-Up: Location-based moderated usability testing

    • During the test session, the moderator will connect with the test participant using MS Teams
    • The moderator will ask to share the test participants’ audio, and camera and share
    • Think-aloud test will be conducted

    Data Collection: The following data are to be collected for this test session.

    • Task Time
    • Completion Rate
    • No. of Errors/Mistakes
    • Usability Problems
    • Test Level questionnaire (Satisfaction rating)

    Key Takeaways

    Challenges

    • Collaborating with teammates who had differing opinions and work styles was initially difficult, but it taught me the value of adaptability and open-mindedness.
    • Realized the importance of initiating action in group projects to keep the team on track and ensure progress, even though it sometimes made me feel unlikeable.
    • Recognized the significance of thorough preparation before meetings with higher-ups, ensuring productive and purposeful discussions.
    • Keeping team members updated on each other's responsibilities proved essential for smooth transitions and maintaining a collaborative team environment.

    Lesson Learned

    • Adaptability and open-mindedness are key when collaborating with people who have differing opinions and work styles.
    • Initiating action and keeping the project moving forward is vital for success, even if it occasionally feels uncomfortable.
    • I learned the importance of thorough preparation before meeting with higher-ups, such as lecturers, supervisors, and clients. Being well-prepared proved to be more effective than attending meetings without a clear purpose, leading to more productive and meaningful discussions.
    • Regular communication and responsibility tracking within the team help maintain cohesion and ensure that the project moves forward smoothly, even when unexpected challenges arise.
